Australia’s Internet Reliance

A visual story about where the nbn network is ready, where it is actually being used, and why fixed-line broadband still carries the weight of Australia’s online life.

Audience: everyday Australians comparing broadband access and performance.

Key message: availability, adoption, speed and demand are related — but they do not move at the same pace.

Why this matters

Internet access is now basic infrastructure for study, work, entertainment and public services. The charts below combine national download-volume data, measured nbn plan speeds, and state-level network premises data to show a simple story: Australia has built broad broadband reach, but the lived experience depends on take-up, technology mix and plan speed.

1

Access: ready does not always mean active

The broadband story starts unevenly across the map. Activation is not just about where the network reaches; it also shows where households and businesses have taken up a service.

A normalised bar view makes the gap visible: in every state, a sizeable share of premises is ready but not yet active.

The lollipop chart ranks activation clearly without overloading the reader with table values.

Technology mix matters. Satellite and fixed wireless serve smaller shares nationally, but they are much more important outside the largest eastern states.

The largest states dominate the absolute numbers, while activation rate and satellite exposure add context.

2

Performance: plan labels are only part of the story

Once connected, plan speed becomes the everyday experience. Measured speeds cluster close to plan labels, but high-speed tiers show larger absolute gaps.

A slope chart highlights that measured speeds were slightly lower in 2022 for most plans in this dataset.

Bullet bars compare measured 2022 performance against the advertised plan label in a compact way.

3

Demand: Australians keep downloading more

Demand kept climbing: total downloads rose from 3.0 million TB to 3.8 million TB across one year.

Despite mobile and wireless growth, fixed-line connections carried the overwhelming majority of download volume.

Indexing reveals the hidden pattern: wireless grew faster in percentage terms, but remained much smaller in volume.

The closing view reinforces the main message: Australia’s digital life is increasingly heavy, fixed-line, and speed-sensitive.
